What Causes Depression:

- Brain chemistry: abnormalities in brain chemical levels may lead to depression.
- Genetics: if you have relatives with depression, you maybe more likely to become depressed.
- Life events: physical and sexual abuse, stress, bullying, the death of loved ones, isolation, and lack of support.
- Medical conditions: physical pain and illness can cause depression, conditions like diabetes and cancer.
- Medications: some medications have depression as a side effect. Drugs and alcohol also cause depression.
- Personality: people who are easily overwhelmed or have trouble copying maybe be prone to depression.
- Hormone levels: changes in female hormones, oestrogen and progesterone during periods of time can raise the risk of depression.

Symptoms include:

- feeling very sad, hopeless or worried.
- not enjoying things that used to give you joy.
- being easily irritated or frustrated.
- Eating too much or too little.
- Changes on how much you sleep.
- experiencing physical problems like headache and stomach-ache.
- thinking about hurting or killing yourself.

The treatment:

- Self-care: regular exercise, enough sleep, spending time with people you care about.
- Psychotherapy
- Alternative medicine
- Medication
- Brain stimulation therapy
